OUR BREWERY

BREWING

The brewing of Kwartje beers starts with pouring water into the brewing kettle. First, malt (sprouted barley) is added, which is ground with a malt mill. We always use two types of malt, namely always pale malt and when we brew dark beers, dark roasted malt is also added to the brewing kettle. The brew is gradually heated to about 78 째 C over an hour and a half. This process is also known as "mashing". During this process, the malt is converted into fermentable sugars. Fact: for our Witbier we use pale malt and wheat.

FILTERING

In a second phase, the brew is pumped to the lauter tun and for 2 hours we separate the liquid part (namely the wort) from the marc (namely the solid grain residues from the malt). The trot ends up in a silo and is collected by a local farmer who offers it as animal feed to his animals. The wort now forms the basis for our beer.

COOKING

The wort ends up in the boiling kettle and a number of ingredients are added, namely hops and possibly also sugar and herbs, depending on the recipe. We use two types of hops: Hallertau Magnum for the bitterness and Kent Golding for the specific aromas. The wort boils for about 90 minutes. The solid particles in the whirlpool are removed from the wort by means of the centrifugal force. That way the heavier particles are pushed towards the center of the kettle and we can start removing the lighter wort from the side of the whirlpool. We quickly cool the boiled wort to about 24 째 C. Since we brew top fermentation beers, this temperature is higher than, for example, with lager or bottom fermented beers where the temperature is about 10 to 14 째 C. that we use for this, then heats up and is recovered in the hot water tank, which thus serves as the basis for the next brew.

FERMENTING

After cooling, the wort goes to the fermentation tank where yeast is added. The yeast converts the sugars in the wort into alcohol and carbon dioxide. This process is called main fermentation and takes about 7-10 days, depending on the type of beer. Fermentation takes place at a temperature of 25 째 C, this high temperature means that we speak of top fermentation beers. In a final phase, the beer matures for a number of weeks in a lagertank. This maturation of the beer is very important because the taste is further refined during this process. Depending on the type of beer, the lagering takes 4 to 8 weeks. Here too there is a difference between top fermentation beers and low fermentation beers, with top fermentation the fermentation takes place at the top of the tank. Once the yeast has worked, it will settle to the bottom of the lager tank.

BRIGHTENING

When the beer has matured long enough, the yeast is removed by means of the centrifuge or, in other words, "mashed". Eventually the beer ends up in the clear beer tank.
